Rationale

What this system does, where it stops, and why.

The Unlock

What the Human Can Now Do

Every payments company shipped agent infrastructure in 2025 — Stripe, Visa, Mastercard, PayPal, Coinbase. No brokerage has. Wealthgate fills that gap: it makes investing programmable for AI agents the way Stripe made payments programmable for developers.

Wealthsimple clients connect their own AI agents — Claude, ChatGPT, or custom — to their investment accounts through Wealthgate. The system is the gatekeeper for vibe-investing: it catalogs every agent action and keeps the account holder in the decision loop for every material trade. Portfolio analysis, tax-loss harvesting, and rebalancing happen at machine speed — then route through an Approval Dashboard where the client reviews full agent reasoning, modifies proposals, and approves or rejects with a tap. Notification channels (push, SMS, WhatsApp) ensure time-sensitive approvals never get missed.

This is not a chatbot. AI agents don't see a UI — they call tools over MCP. Humans see the dashboard. The boundary is explicit, the way Sam Talasila described it: agent access excludes raw account data and withdrawal authority by design.

Agent Capabilities

What AI Agents Can Do Through This System

  • Connect via MCP protocol — agents discover and call tools over JSON-RPC, the same standard adopted by Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, and VS Code. Multi-agent coordination is native: a financial advisor, a portfolio rebalancer, and a tax optimizer can all operate on the same account simultaneously
  • Read portfolios with live TSX/NYSE market data — across Canadian account types (TFSA, RRSP, FHSA, Non-Registered) with contribution room awareness
  • Propose trades with detailed reasoning chains visible in the dashboard
  • Identify tax-loss harvesting opportunities with Canadian superficial loss rule awareness
  • Calculate multi-trade rebalances with target allocation drift detection
  • Search the investment universe across ETFs and stocks with live pricing

Every action is logged with agent identity, parameters, authorization tier, and latency — creating the regulatory-grade audit trail required by CSA Notice 11-348 and OSFI E-23.

Boundaries

Where AI Must Stop

  • Tier 2 ($100–$5,000): queued for account holder approval — the client sees full agent reasoning before deciding
  • Tier 3 (over $5,000): mandatory 15-minute cooling period before the client can approve — no impulsive decisions on large positions
  • Tier 4 (withdrawals, account modifications, closures): permanently blocked in code — no agent, no override, no exception
BLOCKED_OPERATIONS = {"withdraw", "withdraw_funds", "modify_account", "close_account", "transfer"}

These boundaries are enforced in code, not in prompts. No amount of prompt engineering can bypass a Tier 4 block — the operation simply doesn't exist in the agent's tool set.

The Thesis Moment

The Critical Decision That Must Remain Human

The ultimate decision about your future and your investments is still fully human — and personal — and we will make sure it stays that way.

No system should dictate what you do with your money, or put you in a situation of financial stress due to unleashed infrastructure. That principle shapes every architectural decision in Wealthgate.

Here's how it works in practice. Proposal WS-2026-00004: an agent detected the S&P 500 dropped 12% and proposed selling VFV.TO — a $16,230 position. The agent's own analysis showed that selling during >10% drawdowns has negative expected value 73% of the time over 5-year horizons. So it did exactly what a well-designed agent should do — it flagged, it analyzed, and it deferred:

"I recommend human review because factors beyond my analysis include personal risk tolerance and upcoming cash needs."

The Approval Dashboard showed the account holder everything: the agent's reasoning, the dollar amount, the authorization tier, the historical analysis. The account holder rejected the panic-sell:

"Client has 30+ year horizon. Holding through volatility is the right call. Good flag by the agent."

This is Wealthgate's thesis in a single interaction. The agent provides analysis at machine speed. The human — who knows their life goals, their cash needs next month, their emotional state, their time horizon — makes the call. The two-way audit record captures both sides: what the agent recommended, and why the human decided differently. That's not a limitation of AI. That's the system working exactly as designed.

Scale Risks

What Would Break First at Scale

  • The approval queue: as more clients connect agents, the volume of Tier 2/3 proposals scales with the user base — the single-user dashboard needs team-based routing, SLA timers, and escalation paths
  • Notification delivery: push notifications, SMS, and WhatsApp channels need reliable delivery with SLA guarantees — a delayed notification on a time-sensitive trade during market volatility creates real financial risk
  • The database: SQLite needs to move to PostgreSQL for concurrent agent writes across thousands of accounts
  • Auto-approval threshold calibration: the adjustable threshold on the dashboard reduces review volume, but the security tradeoff requires careful tuning per client risk profile — a one-size-fits-all ceiling doesn't work at Wealthsimple scale
