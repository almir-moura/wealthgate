Wealthgate

Agent-Native Investment Infrastructure

What this system does, where it stops, and why.

The Unlock

What the Human Can Now Do

Every payments company shipped agent infrastructure in 2025 — Stripe, Visa, Mastercard, PayPal, Coinbase. No brokerage has. Wealthgate fills that gap: it makes investing programmable for AI agents the way Stripe made payments programmable for developers.

Wealthsimple clients connect their own AI agents — Claude, ChatGPT, or custom — to their investment accounts through Wealthgate. The system is the gatekeeper for vibe-investing: it catalogs every agent action and keeps the account holder in the decision loop for every material trade.

Agent Capabilities

What AI Agents Can Do Through This System

Agents connect via MCP protocol — the same standard adopted by Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, and VS Code. They can read portfolios with live market data, propose trades with reasoning, identify tax-loss harvesting opportunities, calculate multi-trade rebalances, and search the investment universe. Multi-agent coordination is native: a financial advisor, a rebalancer, and a tax optimizer can all operate on the same account simultaneously.

Boundaries

Where AI Must Stop

Tier 2 ($100–$5,000) queues for account holder approval. Tier 3 (over $5,000) adds a mandatory 15-minute cooling period. Tier 4 (withdrawals, account modifications, closures) is permanently blocked in code — no agent, no override, no exception. These boundaries are enforced in code, not in prompts.

The Thesis Moment

The Critical Decision That Must Remain Human

The ultimate decision about your future and your investments is still fully human — and personal — and we will make sure it stays that way.

No system should dictate what you do with your money, or put you in a situation of financial stress due to unleashed infrastructure. In this demo, an agent flagged a 12% market crash and proposed a $16,230 sell — but deferred to the human because personal risk tolerance, cash needs, and life context are beyond any model's reach. The account holder rejected the panic-sell. The system worked exactly as designed.

Scale Risks

What Would Break First at Scale

The approval queue becomes the bottleneck as clients connect agents at scale. Notification delivery (push, SMS, WhatsApp) needs SLA guarantees. SQLite needs PostgreSQL for concurrent writes. The auto-approval threshold needs per-client calibration.
